Japanese Fruit Cake III

The perfect cake for the holidays

This Japanese Fruit Cake recipe is easy to read, easier to follow, and easiest to eat!

It is moist and dense with fruit and flavor. The perfect dessert for the holidays or just about any day.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup butter
  • 2 cups white sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 cup chopped raisins
  • 1 cup walnuts
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1(8 ounce) can crushed pineapple with juice
  • 2 cups flaked coconut
  • 2 cups white sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons grated lemon zest
  • 2 lemons, juiced
  • 1 1/2 cups water

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 150 degrees C (300 degrees F).
  2. Grease and flour 3-9 inch pans.
  3. Sift together and set aside:
    • 3 cups flour
    • baking soda
    • cinnamon
    • allspice
    • nutmeg
  4. In a large bowl, cream together 1 cup butter and 2 cups sugar until light and fluffy.
  5. Add eggs one at a time, beating well with each addition.
  6. Add the flour mixture alternately with the buttermilk; beat well.
  7. Fold in raisins and chopped nuts.
  8. Pour batter into three 9-inch pans and bake in preheated oven for 55 to 60 minutes. Allow to cool.
  9. In a large saucepan, combine:
    • water
    • pineapple
    • coconut
    • 2 1/2 cups sugar
    • 2 tablespoons flour
    • lemon zest
    • lemon juice
  10. Bring to a boil, stirring constantly until thickened.
  11. Let cool and spread between each layer and on top of cake.
